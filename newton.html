Newton's Cube: Physics Escape Room



Link to the game


In Newton's Cube, you will be transported to a sci-fi world where you must use your knowledge of physics to escape the puzzles and challenges of an escape room.


As you progress through the game, you'll encounter a range of physics-based scenarios that will test your understanding of real-world physics concepts.


To help bring our sci-fi world to life, we've explored the Unreal Engine marketplace for potential game assets that can enhance the immersive experience of Newton's Cube.

Planning & Execution


Making the Right Choice: How We Decided on a Game Engine for Our Project



We used a decision matrix table to evaluate game engine options based on experience ,3D capabilities, price, physics capability, and available assets. After considering these factors, we selected Unreal Engine for its strong 3D capabilities and available assets, despite some team members having less experience with it. Our careful planning and execution ensured the success of our project.


Finding the Target Audience / Social research

To ensure our game resonates with our intended audience, we conducted research to determine who our target audience is. We considered various factors such as age, gender, interests, and preferences. We conducted surveys, gathered feedback from focus groups, and analyzed market trends.



By doing so, we were able to gain valuable insights into our target audience and tailor our game accordingly. Our goal was to create an experience that not only educates but also engages and entertains our target audience. The process of finding the target audience helped us to ensure that our game meets the needs of our intended audience and offers an enjoyable and educational experience.


Topics we decided to use for our game

  • Newton's Law
  • Momentum
  • Projectile Motion
  • Energy
  • Electricity

Gamification Process

As part of our gamification process, we transformed complex physics topics into a real escape room puzzle game. We carefully planned out each level and studied each topic to ensure that the puzzles accurately represented the principles of physics.


To provide players with assistance in case they get stuck, we also created a user manual that offers helpful tips and guidance. Our goal was to create an interactive and engaging experience that not only taught physics concepts but also encouraged critical thinking and problem-solving skills. By providing players with a user manual, we hope to help them overcome any obstacles they may encounter in the game and ensure they have an enjoyable and educational experience.


Level Design

When it came to designing the levels for our game, we used the same engine that we planned to use for the final product. By doing so, we were able to create accurate 1 to 1 scale mock-ups of each level, which allowed us to plan out the interactions and functionality without the need for coding.


We also implemented a peer-review process, where each team member reviewed and critiqued each other's level designs to ensure that they met our standards for quality and engagement. By carefully designing each level, we aimed to create an immersive and challenging experience that would reinforce the physics concepts that we wanted to teach
